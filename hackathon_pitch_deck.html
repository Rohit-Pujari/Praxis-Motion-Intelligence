Praxis | Human Movement Intelligence for Rehab and Recovery
Problem

Most movement demos stop at pose detection or a single score

Typical systems can say:

  • where the body landmarks are
  • what the rough motion score is
  • whether something looks abnormal

But clinicians, coaches, and judges want more:

  • What does normal movement look like?
  • How far is the user from that baseline?
  • Is this mild recovery deviation or severe limitation?
  • Which joints are contributing most?

Praxis answers those questions with a dataset-driven and visually explainable pipeline.

Solution

UCF101 stays the core. Other datasets interpret severity.

Primary baseline
UCF101

Defines how movement should ideally be performed.

Moderate deviation
Injury Profile

Calibrated from the sports injury CSV in the project root.

Severe deviation
Stroke Profile

Anchored from stroke MATLAB threshold data.

Key design rule: everyone is compared against normal movement first.

Injury and stroke references are not replacement baselines. They are interpretation layers used to decide whether the deviation pattern looks like recovery-level limitation or severe neurological restriction.

Slide 3
How It Works

Fast pipeline, no heavy training

  1. Upload video, record webcam clip, or paste landmark JSON
  2. Extract pose with MediaPipe
  3. Compute joint angles for shoulders, elbows, hips, and knees
  4. Score mobility, symmetry, and smoothness
  1. Compare against UCF101-derived normal profile
  2. Interpret severity with injury and stroke profiles
  3. Return joint status and overall condition
  4. Generate explainable replay and feedback

Output includes: joint status, overall condition, event timeline, joint charts, repetition summary, human-readable feedback, and a stickman overlay colored by severity.

Slide 5
Judge Demo Flow

How to present this in under 2 minutes

  1. State the problem: pose estimation alone is not enough.
  2. Show that UCF101 is the normal movement reference.
  3. Upload or record a movement clip.
  4. Run analysis with a selected condition profile.
  5. Show the overall score and overall condition.
  6. Show joint-level status and human-readable feedback.
  7. Play the colored stickman overlay and event timeline.

Final message to judges: Praxis transforms raw human pose into movement intelligence that is explainable, visual, and useful for rehab and recovery.
